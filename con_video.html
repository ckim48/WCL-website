Featured Speakers

Lauren Hahm

She is an undergraduate student at Ewha Womans University, pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in International Studies. Lauren has worked in several companies including Samsung Economic Research Institute and Lufthansa German Airlines. Most recently, she finished her internship at a broadcasting company. She has grown an interest in international law and wishes to attend law school post-graduation. Lauren will be presenting on the female portrayal in media, touching base on how female characters have been depicted and consumed in the past and how far we have come. She also wishes to address the need for a more diverse female presence and leadership in the industry.

Amani Kurtz

My name is Amani L. Kurtz, I'm in the class of 2021. I am the former Round Square leader, Vice-President of the Black and Brown Student Union, and recently the intern for the Gandhi Smriti and Darshan Samiti Foundation. My presentation will be about recognizing the need for diversity among leaders, both neurologically and racially and the importance of not only equality but equity as well while leading. Neurologically as in having leaders that are both neurotypical and neurodivergent. The importance of this is that it creates a better environment to not only lead but also create leaders. Additionally, equity is important in leading. It gives an opportunity for leaders and co-leaders to focus on the needs of those they’re leading. Equity allows leaders to make sure that resources and support are divided to support individual needs within their group.
