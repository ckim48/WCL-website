Featured Speakers

Lauren Hahm

She is an undergraduate student at Ewha Womans University, pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in International Studies. Lauren has worked in several companies including Samsung Economic Research Institute and Lufthansa German Airlines. Most recently, she finished her internship at a broadcasting company. She has grown an interest in international law and wishes to attend law school post-graduation. Lauren will be presenting on the female portrayal in media, touching base on how female characters have been depicted and consumed in the past and how far we have come. She also wishes to address the need for a more diverse female presence and leadership in the industry.

Crystal Coser

Crystal Coser is a Chadwick school alumni and Harvard university graduate, now the owner of catering company ‘Bites and Bashes’ and Associate Editor of an online magazine ‘Eater’.

Amani Kurtz

My name is Amani L. Kurtz, I'm in the class of 2021. I am the former Round Square leader, Vice-President of the Black and Brown Student Union, and recently the intern for the Gandhi Smriti and Darshan Samiti Foundation. My presentation will be about recognizing the need for diversity among leaders, both neurologically and racially and the importance of not only equality but equity as well while leading. Neurologically as in having leaders that are both neurotypical and neurodivergent. The importance of this is that it creates a better environment to not only lead but also create leaders. Additionally, equity is important in leading. It gives an opportunity for leaders and co-leaders to focus on the needs of those they’re leading. Equity allows leaders to make sure that resources and support are divided to support individual needs within their group.

Valentine Breitbarth

You have your own unique brand of values, gifts, and experience. These are your building blocks for powerful leadership. You have the power to lead by example, to lift up those around you, and to be the change you wish to see in the world just by choosing how you interact with others and the energy you bring. In this session we will discuss how to identify and center around your personal values, the core premises of energy leadership, and tangible ways to step into to more conscious interaction with the world around you. How will you use your superpower?

Valentine Breitbarth is a public health professional and life & leadership coach and brings experience from the nonprofit, healthcare, and education sectors. She is also a daughter, wife, mother, and friend.

Nwakego Ibrahim Pam

She is a seasoned expatriate spouse, an Educationist, Linguist, Inspirational Speaker, transformational Leader, Social Entrepreneur, Mentor, Employer, Trainer, Philanthropist. and an Administrator.

Princess Nwakego Ibrahim-Pam is the Founder and Chairperson of the Expat Professional and Business Women's Network International. She is also the chief Lead Consultant at the EPBWN Consultants International. She is the co-founder of the Spousal Association of the Green Climate Fund.

She received Awards from IFEZ and the Nigerian Government. She is a member of the Ifez Community Consultative Committee. She is a member of the International Society of Female Professionals (ISFP).

Seblewongel Negussie

My name is Seblewongel, the Gender and Social Specialist at the Green Climate Fund. My husband and our two daughters- who attend Chadwick International - moved to South Korea three years ago and we love it here.

My work to date is about highlighting the importance of addressing gender inequality issues and to address the barriers to equality. Our engagement at the GCF is about, ensuring equal opportunities and benefits are provided to both women and men, as impacts of climate change impact all. We also acknowledge the differentiated and disproportionate impacts climate change has on women and have a policy to address that.

I strongly believe that leadership should be inclusive. Each one of us are leaders in our own rights. Some of us will be known across the globe, in our respective countries, in our communities and some of us in our schools, homes and amongst our peers. Some of us do not consider ourselves as leaders, but spend our lives raising, supporting and nurturing leaders. Some have the enabling environment to be leaders I consider those of us here have better opportunity to acknowledge, discover and nurture the leader in us. My presentation tries to draw a parallel between how in a school, leaders are nurtured and how we at the GCF are trying to contribute towards nurturing leaders, particularly women leaders, through our investments.
